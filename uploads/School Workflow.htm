School Workflow & Requirements Blueprint
Online Olympiad / Talent Exam Portal

1. Executive Summary

This document serves as the foundational product-planning and UX-design blueprint for the School user journey within the Online Olympiad / Talent Exam Portal. It explicitly details functional requirements, user workflows, and structural dependencies required to facilitate school engagement. The scope focuses entirely on schools acting as analytics, reporting, and coordination stakeholders, intentionally excluding direct exam operations and parent workflows.

2. Role of School in the Portal Ecosystem

In the V1 ecosystem, the School is primarily an analytics and reporting consumer rather than an exam administrator. The burden of registration, authentication, exam-taking, and proctoring lies directly with the individual student. The school's role is to track student participation, review aggregated and individual performance metrics, compare school-wide results against global percentiles, and optionally coordinate custom examination windows or sponsor future payments. The school portal exists to validate the school's involvement and reward them with actionable insights.

3. Key Assumptions and Scope Boundaries

  • Exclusions: Parent workflows are entirely excluded from this document and the V1 school portal scope.
  • Student Independence: Students perform direct individual signups using an Aadhaar-linked mobile OTP (via a compliant KYC provider).
  • Exam Operations: Proctoring is AI-assisted for students. Schools do not manually proctor or intervene in live exam sessions.
  • Result Timeline: Results are not instant; they are released to the school dashboard only after rigorous review and normalization by portal administrators.
  • Language and Platform: English only. Accessible via a responsive web application (dedicated school dashboard interface).
  • Support: Schools will have access to a dedicated helpdesk for institutional queries.

4. School User Roles and Permissions

Role Name Description Key Permissions
School Coordinator Primary administrative contact for the institution. Manages the school profile and coordinates with portal admins. Activate account, update profile, view participating students, track slots, request custom windows, download all reports, handle future sponsorships.
Read-only Reporting User Secondary user (e.g., specific teachers, principal) requiring access to performance data without administrative capabilities. View analytics dashboards, view participation summary, download exportable reports. Cannot modify profile or request custom windows.

5. End-to-End School Journey Overview

The standard journey for a school engaging with the platform follows a distinct linear progression through the exam lifecycle:

  1. Onboarding: School receives an official invitation link, activates the account, and sets up the institution profile.
  2. Pre-Exam Monitoring: School tracks which of its students have successfully registered via direct signup and monitors their slot allocations.
  3. Coordination (Optional): School Coordinator requests a custom exam window if standard slots do not align with the academic calendar.
  4. Post-Exam Consumption: Following exam normalization, the school accesses detailed analytics, performance comparisons, and exportable reports.
  5. Future Operations: School engages in bulk payment sponsorship for students (slated for Phase 2).

6 & 7. Detailed School Workflow by Stage

Stage A: Invitation and Account Activation

  • Objective: Securely onboard authorized school representatives onto the portal.
  • Primary Actions: Click email invitation link, set password, agree to institutional terms of service.
  • System Behavior: Validates invitation token, provisions school tenant space, assigns 'School Coordinator' role to the inviter email.
  • Data Shown: Welcome screen, terms of service, password creation form.
  • Notifications: Account activation success email.
  • Edge Cases / Blind Spots: Invitation link expires; email filtered to spam; unauthorized staff member attempts activation.
  • UX Notes: Use a clean, distraction-free landing page. Clearly state the security requirements for the password.

Stage B: Institution Profile Setup

  • Objective: Capture accurate metadata about the school for proper categorization and reporting.
  • Primary Actions: Input school details (board affiliation, address, total student count), upload school logo.
  • System Behavior: Saves profile data, updates dashboard header with school branding.
  • Data Shown: Forms for basic institutional data.
  • Notifications: None.
  • Edge Cases / Blind Spots: Mismatch between provided school name and the name students select during their direct signup.
  • UX Notes: Make secondary fields optional to reduce onboarding friction.

Stage C: Dashboard Onboarding

  • Objective: Orient the user to the portal's capabilities.
  • Primary Actions: Dismiss welcome modal, navigate side menu.
  • System Behavior: Displays blank-state or sample data if no students have registered yet.
  • Data Shown: Navigation menu, quick stats widgets (currently zeroed out), support helpdesk widget.
  • Notifications: In-app tooltip tour (optional).
  • Edge Cases / Blind Spots: Empty dashboard might confuse users; clear messaging needed explaining that students must sign up independently.
  • UX Notes: Provide a "Share Registration Link" quick action to help schools distribute the correct signup URL to their students.

Stage D: Student Participation Monitoring

  • Objective: Allow schools to track which students have registered for the Olympiad under their institution.
  • Primary Actions: Filter student list by grade/class, search by student name.
  • System Behavior: Queries student database where "School = [Institution ID]".
  • Data Shown: Table containing Student Name, Grade, Registration Status, KYC verification status (abstracted).
  • Notifications: Weekly summary email of new registrations.
  • Edge Cases / Blind Spots: Students typo their school name during direct signup resulting in missing records on the school dashboard.
  • UX Notes: Include an "Unrecognized Student" or "Claim Student" dispute mechanism if a student is missing from the roster.

Stage E: Slot Allocation Tracking

  • Objective: Provide visibility into when students are scheduled to take the exam.
  • Primary Actions: View calendar or list of upcoming exam slots for registered students.
  • System Behavior: Aggregates exam schedules from the student database.
  • Data Shown: Date, time, number of students scheduled per slot.
  • Notifications: Alert if a large percentage of students miss their scheduled slot.
  • Edge Cases / Blind Spots: Last-minute student rescheduling causing discrepancies in school tracking.
  • UX Notes: Provide a simple aggregate view (e.g., "50 students scheduled for Oct 12").

Stage F: Custom Exam Window Request

  • Objective: Accommodate schools that need a dedicated timeframe for their students to take the exam from the school lab.
  • Primary Actions: Submit request form with preferred dates, time, and estimated student volume.
  • System Behavior: Logs a ticket/request to the Portal Admin dashboard. Status marked as 'Pending'.
  • Data Shown: Form with date pickers; status tracker of past requests.
  • Notifications: Confirmation of request submission; approval/rejection email from Admins.
  • Edge Cases / Blind Spots: Requesting a window that overlaps with platform maintenance; requesting a window after the standard exam period ends.
  • UX Notes: Clearly state the SLA (Service Level Agreement) for request reviews (e.g., "Allow 48 hours for admin approval").

Stage G: Results and Analytics Consumption

  • Objective: Deliver actionable insights post-exam normalization.
  • Primary Actions: View aggregate scores, percentile benchmarking, and school-comparison charts.
  • System Behavior: Unlocks analytics module only after portal admins publish the normalized results.
  • Data Shown: Participation summary, grade/class-wise performance, student-wise score details, global vs. school percentile charts.
  • Notifications: Email notification: "Your School's Olympiad Results are Ready".
  • Edge Cases / Blind Spots: Results withheld for specific students due to AI-proctoring flags, leading to incomplete data for the school.
  • UX Notes: Clearly label any students whose results are "Pending Review" so the school understands the data gap.

Stage H: Report Download and Sharing

  • Objective: Enable offline analysis and physical record-keeping.
  • Primary Actions: Export data to CSV/Excel, download formatted PDF summary reports.
  • System Behavior: Generates asynchronous file downloads for large datasets.
  • Data Shown: Download history, file generation progress indicator.
  • Notifications: In-app alert when a large report is ready for download.
  • Edge Cases / Blind Spots: Extremely large datasets timing out during CSV generation.
  • UX Notes: Use standard, widely accepted formats. Ensure PDF reports carry the portal's official branding.

Stage I: Future Sponsorship / Payment Workflow (Later Phase)

  • Objective: Allow the school to pay for bulk student registrations via invoice.
  • Primary Actions: Generate invoice based on student count, upload payment proof.
  • System Behavior: Generates unique payment links or offline invoice PDFs.
  • Data Shown: Financial ledger, invoice history, payment status.
  • Notifications: Payment receipt confirmation.
  • Edge Cases / Blind Spots: Partial payments; refunds for students who ultimately do not take the exam.
  • UX Notes: Keep this section hidden or marked "Coming Soon" for MVP to avoid confusion.

Stage J: Support and Escalation

  • Objective: Provide institutional support via a dedicated helpdesk.
  • Primary Actions: Raise a support ticket, view ticket status, reply to helpdesk agent.
  • System Behavior: Integrates with internal Admin support ticketing system.
  • Data Shown: Ticket ID, Subject, Status (Open/Closed), Message thread.
  • Notifications: Email updates when an agent replies to a ticket.
  • Edge Cases / Blind Spots: Schools raising tickets for individual student technical issues (which students should handle directly).
  • UX Notes: Include a disclaimer before ticket submission reminding schools that student Aadhaar/OTP issues must be handled by the student portal.

8. Page / Screen Inventory for the School Dashboard

  • Authentication: Login Page, Set Password Page, Forgot Password.
  • Setup: Institution Profile Settings, User Management (add Read-only users).
  • Dashboard Home: Overview metrics, quick links, recent notifications.
  • Students Tracker: Paginated list of registered students with search and filters.
  • Exam Schedule: Calendar/List view of student slot allocations.
  • Window Requests: Form to request custom dates, list of past requests.
  • Analytics - Overview: School-wide performance graphs and benchmarking.
  • Analytics - Class/Grade: Drill-down views by specific cohorts.
  • Analytics - Student Detail: Individual performance scorecards.
  • Reports Center: Interface for generating and downloading CSV/PDF exports.
  • Helpdesk: Ticket creation form and active ticket list.

9. Functional Requirements List (Grouped by Module)

  • Identity & Access Module:
    • System must support token-based email invitations for school onboarding.
    • System must support role-based access control (Coordinator vs. Read-only).
  • Data Mapping Module:
    • System must dynamically link independent student signups to the correct institution profile based on selected school codes/names.
  • Reporting Engine:
    • System must calculate and display school averages against regional/national percentiles.
    • System must export data sets securely to CSV and PDF formats.
  • Request Management Module:
    • System must provide a workflow for submitting, tracking, and updating custom exam window requests.

10. Non-functional / Operational Considerations

  • Data Privacy: The portal must ensure that read-only users cannot access personally identifiable information beyond what is necessary for academic reporting.
  • Scalability: The reporting engine must handle high loads gracefully, especially immediately after results are normalized and published when traffic spikes are expected.
  • Responsive Design: While analytics are best viewed on desktop, the dashboard must be fully accessible and readable on mobile web browsers for principals/coordinators on the go.
  • Data Integrity: Strict database constraints must be applied so a school cannot view analytics for students enrolled in a different institution.

11. Risks, Blind Spots, and Unresolved Design Questions

Risk - Student-to-School Mapping: Because students sign up directly, they may select the wrong school, misspell the school name, or fail to enter a school code. This will result in inaccurate school analytics.
Mitigation idea: Implement a standardized drop-down list or unique school invite codes instead of free-text entry during student signup.
Blind Spot - AI Proctoring Communication: Schools may expect to see why a student scored poorly or was disqualified. If AI proctoring flags a student, the school dashboard needs a standardized, legally safe way of indicating "Disqualified due to protocol violation" without exposing raw proctoring data to the school.
Unresolved Question - Read-only User Limits: How many read-only reporting users can a School Coordinator invite? (Recommendation: cap at 5 for V1 to reduce support overhead).

12. School FAQ by Stage

Stage: Onboarding & Dashboard Access

  • Q: How do I activate my school's account? A: Click the secure link sent to your institutional email, create a password, and log in to the portal.
  • Q: Can I add other teachers to view the results? A: Yes, the School Coordinator can invite staff members as 'Read-only Reporting Users' from the profile settings.

Stage: Student Participation & Slot Tracking

  • Q: Why isn't a specific student showing up on my dashboard? A: The student may not have completed their direct signup, or they may have registered under an incorrect school name. Please have the student verify their profile.
  • Q: Can I change a student's exam slot? A: No. Since students register independently, they must manage their own slot rescheduling via the student portal.

Stage: Custom Exam Windows

  • Q: What is a custom exam window? A: It is a dedicated time block requested by the school to allow students to take the exam together in a school computer lab.
  • Q: How far in advance must I request a custom window? A: Requests must be submitted at least 7 working days prior to the desired date.

Stage: Analytics & Reports

  • Q: When will I see the students' results? A: Results will populate on your dashboard only after the official exam period ends and portal admins have completed the review and normalization process.
  • Q: Can I download a list of all participating students and their scores? A: Yes, navigate to the Reports Center to download CSV or PDF summaries.

Stage: Sponsored Payments (Future) & Support

  • Q: Can the school pay for a batch of 50 students? A: Bulk invoicing will be available in a future update. Currently, students must complete payments individually.
  • Q: How do I contact support if the dashboard isn't updating? A: Use the Helpdesk tab to raise a support ticket directly with our administration team.

13. Recommended MVP vs Later Phases

Feature / Module Phase 1 (MVP) Phase 2 / Phase 3
Account Onboarding Invite-only via portal admin email link. Single Coordinator role. Self-serve institutional signup. Multiple custom roles.
Student Tracking View list of registered students and their scheduled slot times. Automated discrepancy alerts; bulk "claim student" dispute tools.
